The Tank

Golden Nugget
129 Fremont St
Las Vegas, NV 89101

The Tank is a hotel pool that allows you swim with sharks. Includes a waterslide, cabanas and daybeds, and an H2O bar. It is open to people of all age, and kids under 3 are allowed for free, while non-hotel guests pay between $25 to $50.

Rick Harrison's Gold & Silver Pawn Shop

713 Las Vegas Blvd S
Las Vegas, NV 89101

As the origin of the famous show Pawn Stars, Rick Harrison’s Gold & Silver Pawn Shop is well-known and visited by many. Even if you’re not a fan of the show, the pawn shop has its own appeal, with the many items you could find.

More Information: Official Website

Reviews: Yelp

The Neon Museum

770 Las Vegas Blvd N
Las Vegas, NV 89101

The Neon Museum is a place to view iconic Las Vegas signs. Take an hour-long guided tour to see the signs, sign up for photo walks, or book the site for a special event to see the site of the signs. Guided tours range from $15 to $30.

Pickleball

The Plaza
1 S. Main St.
Las Vegas, NV 89101

The Plaza Hotel and Casino (1 Main Street) offers unlimited games of pickleball for cheap. Hotel guests play for free, with Las Vegas locals playing for $3 and non-hotel guests paying $5. The courts are open daily.

More Information: Official Website

Reviews: Las Vegas Review-Journal

  • Category: Downtown, Entertainment ($)

Downtown Delight Foodie Tour

Downtown

This foodie tour takes you through the best foods and sights of Downtown. Go through Fremont Street, see the world’s largest golden nugget, and more for a price of $75. If this is not your cup of tea, the site offers many more foodie tours in Las Vegas with varying prices. Make sure to free up your schedule as the tour has a 2-3 hour duration, and dress for the weather. Claim your spot ahead of time as reservations go by fast!

More Information: Official Website

Reviews: TripAdvisor

  • Category: Downtown, Entertainment ($)

The Writer's Block

1020 Fremont St
Las Vegas, NV 89101

The Writer’s Block is not your ordinary bookstore. Located at 1020 Fremont Street, this place hosts many different things, including multitudes of books, a writer’s studio, field trips and classes, and author visits and artist performances.
